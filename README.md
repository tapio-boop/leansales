# Lean Sales — Website

The website for the Lean Sales methodology: a systematic approach to B2B sales built on Lean thinking.

## What is Lean Sales?

Most B2B companies grow sales by adding more — more reps, more outreach, more pipeline. But more activity doesn't mean more results. In fact, the opposite is often true: bloated pipelines, unfocused effort, and unpredictable revenue.

**Lean Sales takes the opposite approach.** Inspired by Lean manufacturing — the same thinking that transformed Toyota, and later software development — Lean Sales focuses on doing fewer things, better:

- **Identify waste** — every activity that doesn't move a deal forward is waste. Generic emails, unqualified meetings, proposals to wrong-fit buyers.
- **Optimize flow** — reduce the time between first contact and closed deal by removing bottlenecks and handoff delays.
- **Build quality in** — qualify rigorously early, so you don't waste weeks on deals that were never real.
- **Learn continuously** — every win and every loss teaches something. Capture it systematically, not anecdotally.

The result: **a sales organization that is predictable, scalable, and focused on what actually drives revenue.**

## The Methodology

Lean Sales was developed by **Tapio Nissilä** and published in the book *Lean Sales — More Sales with Less Selling* (2013). It has since evolved to include a 26-agent AI architecture that operationalizes Lean principles in modern B2B sales.

**Five core principles:**
1. **Customer value first** — every action must create demonstrable value for the buyer
2. **Align to the buying process** — map your selling process to how customers actually decide
3. **Proactive over reactive** — engage before the RFP; proactive sellers win at 60% vs 20% for reactive
4. **Data-driven qualification** — score opportunities on facts, not gut feel
5. **Eliminate waste** — ruthlessly cut activities that don't contribute to outcomes
