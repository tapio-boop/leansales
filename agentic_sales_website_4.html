What is HAR?

Human-Agent Ratio is a two-dimensional framework for designing organizations where humans and AI agents collaborate. It answers the question most companies cannot: for each task in your sales process, what is the right balance between human judgment and AI capability?

Without HAR, companies either over-automate complex tasks (AI makes confident but wrong calls on stakeholder politics) or under-automate simple ones (humans waste time approving routine actions). HAR prevents both failure modes with a structured governance model.

HAR Framework

Four levels of human-AI collaboration — each matched to actual task complexity

HAR uses the Cynefin complexity framework to classify every sales task into its true domain, then assigns the appropriate collaboration model. This is workforce design, not just technology deployment.

Audit
HAR: 10-50+ agents per human
AI executes autonomously. Humans review periodically and handle exceptions. Standard playbooks apply — the relationship between cause and effect is known.
Outreach sequences, lead nurturing, content distribution, customer handoff. The tasks your team shouldn't be spending time on.
Validate
HAR: 5-15 agents per human
AI performs expert analysis. Humans validate before action. Right answers exist but require analytical work to discover.
Market intelligence, qualification scoring, pricing analysis, proposal generation, pipeline forecasting. The analytical work that AI does faster and more consistently.
Direct
HAR: 1-3 agents per human
Humans lead. AI augments perception, surfaces signals, suggests hypotheses. No right answers — only emergent ones discovered through interaction.
Deal strategy, stakeholder navigation, negotiation, sales coaching, discovery conversations. Where deals are actually won or lost.
Full Control
HAR: 0-1 agents per human
Humans decide and act. AI supports after stabilization with rapid information gathering and scenario modeling. Novel situations that demand immediate judgment.
Deal crises — champion departure, budget freeze, competitive disruption, M&A. The situations where playbooks don't apply.

Sales Motions

One system, three go-to-market motions — each with its own HAR profile

The same agent catalogue serves different strategic objectives. HAR determines which agents activate, how much autonomy they get, and how your team interacts with them.

Expand

Grow Revenue Within Existing Accounts

AI agents continuously analyze your customer base to identify expansion opportunities your team is missing — then guide the complex internal selling process.

HAR Profile

Medium overall. Validate on analytics and proposals. Direct oversight on stakeholder navigation and negotiation — expansion politics are Complex domain work.

  • Customer Success agents monitor satisfaction and usage signals
  • Analytics agents identify expansion-ready accounts and timing
  • Deal Strategy agents guide internal selling for expansions
  • Negotiation agents support contract amendments
Infra: CRM (accounts, usage, contracts), Email (relationship signals), Calendar (meeting patterns)
Acquire

Win New Customers in Your Markets

Full-funnel system that shifts HAR levels as deals progress — high automation at top, expert analysis in middle, human-led strategy at bottom where deals are won or lost.

HAR Profile

Audit at top of funnel (high agent ratio). Validate in middle. Direct at bottom (low agent ratio). HAR shifts as deal complexity increases through the pipeline.

  • Market Intel agents identify targets using firmographic and intent signals
  • Outreach agents run personalized sequences (Audit level)
  • Qualification agents score opportunities (Validate level)
  • Deal Strategy agents navigate buying committees (Direct level)
Infra: Full read/write across CRM, Email, Calendar, Documents. Heaviest integration.
Enter

Penetrate New Markets and Segments

Starts in discovery mode with high human involvement. HAR ratio tightens progressively as market patterns emerge and playbooks get validated.

HAR Profile

Direct/Full Control initially — maximum human involvement because everything is unknown. Relaxes to Validate, then Audit over 6-12 months as patterns codify into playbooks.

  • Market Intel agents probe the new landscape in discovery mode
  • Discovery agents run structured experiments with early prospects
  • Win/Loss agents capture every outcome as a learning signal
  • Outreach activates only after validated playbooks exist
Infra: CRM (experiment tracking), Email (response patterns). Writes new ICPs and playbooks.

Foundation

12 years of methodology, now augmented by AI agents

The Agentic Sales System is not a technology looking for a problem. It is the natural evolution of a proven B2B sales methodology — refined through over a decade of enterprise engagements, now embedded into AI agents governed by the HAR framework.

The foundation is Lean Sales, a methodology developed by Tapio Nissilä and published in 2013 as "Lean Sales: The Revolutionary Methodology to Get 10–30% More Sales." Lean Sales introduced systematic, data-driven approaches to B2B sales at a time when most organizations relied on individual heroics and gut-feel forecasting.

The Agentic Sales System takes the Lean Sales principles — systematic qualification, value-driven selling, continuous improvement through measurement — and embeds them into AI agents that operate continuously across your entire pipeline. What was once a methodology that depended on individual discipline is now a system governed by the HAR model.

The HAR (Human-Agent Ratio) model represents the next evolution: a dedicated management framework for designing workforces where humans and AI agents collaborate. It uses the Cynefin complexity framework as a classification tool to ensure each task gets the right type of intelligence and the right level of human oversight. HAR is currently being developed as a standalone management framework applicable beyond sales.
